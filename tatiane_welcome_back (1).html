Part Two

Introduce or Present?

These two verbs are often confused. Let's see the difference clearly.

TO INTRODUCE  /  TO PRESENT

TO INTRODUCE

You introduce someone — you make a new connection between two people who do not know each other yet. You bring them together for the first time.

"Let me introduce my colleague, Sarah."
"I would like to introduce you to our new manager."

TO PRESENT

You present something — a project, a report, an idea — or in very formal contexts, you can present a person to an audience or authority.

"I will present our new product today."
"She will present the results to the board."
The simple rule: Use "introduce" for people meeting people. Use "present" for showing ideas, projects, or data — or in very formal ceremonies.

Part Six

Business Departments — What is your department?

When you introduce yourself professionally, you say which department you work in. Learn the most important ones below.

How to say it: "I work in the Marketing department."  |  "I am in Finance."
💼
Human Resources
Recursos Humanos · RH
Manages hiring, employee welfare, training and company culture.
HR · People
📊
Finance
Financeiro
Controls the company's money: budgets, payments and financial reports.
Numbers · Money
📣
Marketing
Marketing
Promotes products and services, manages brand image and communication.
Brand · Strategy
🤝
Sales
Vendas / Comercial
Sells the company's products or services to clients and partners.
Revenue · Clients
💻
IT
Tecnologia da Informação
Manages computers, networks, software and all technology in the company.
Tech · Systems
⚙️
Operations
Operações
Makes sure the company runs smoothly every day — processes and logistics.
Processes · Flow
📋
Administration
Administração / Administrativo
Organises documents, schedules and the general running of the office.
Organisation
⚖️
Legal
Jurídico
Handles contracts, legal compliance and company law matters.
Law · Contracts
